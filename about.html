Shades of Happiness: Exploring Changes in the World's Happiness Score from 2015-2022

This choropleth map shows the relative happiness score around the world from 2015 through 2022. To interact with the map, you can use the tableau interface. You can scroll left or right to go back and forth between years, or drop the year bar to select a specific year.

The happiness score is represented on the map by shades of blue, with lighter blues indicating lower scores and darker blues indicating higher scores of happiness. You can also click on specific regions to either keep or omit them from the map view.

It is important to note that some countries may not have data for certain years, either because they may have been recently independent or there was no data collection available. Additionally, due to the COVID pandemic, some countries were not able to record data during certain years after 2019.

The data as a whole reveals a downward trend in world happiness over time. This responds to one of our study questions on how the happiness scores of various parts of the world compare through time. While the level of happiness has remained relatively stable in some nations, it has significantly declined in others. Numerous factors, such as current economic conditions, political unrest, and social changes, could be to blame for this. The underlying causes of these shifts can be determined with the aid of more research, which will also assist to guide the development of policies that will increase world happiness.
